LESSON 3 > EXERCISE 9 > LISTENING
LISTEN AGAIN AND READ.

M1: Hey, you’re new here, aren’t you?

M2: That’s right. Actually, it’s my first day, and I’m just getting used to the job. Could I ask you some questions about the people who work here?

M1: Of course. Go ahead.

M2: Well, how’s the boss here? Is he easy to work with?

M1: I think he’s a good manager. He doesn’t get angry easily, and he’s always available to help people.

M2: That’s good. What about the IT people? I need to get a password to use the computer system.

M1: They’ll get on with their work eventually, but they’re not the fastest, really!

M2: What do you mean?

M1: I mean that you need to call them every hour to check what’s happening, or you’ll be waiting for a long time!

M2: Ah, I see. What can you tell me about the other people in the office?

M1: We’re a good team, although some of us are better team players than others.

M2: I’m not sure I follow you.

M1: Well, whoever you’re working with, make sure you can trust them. It can be difficult at first, but you’ll get used to it. Did you hear about the man who delivers the coffees?

M2: No. What about him?

M1: Well, he is very slow too, so make sure you get hold of him as soon as you see him, or you’ll never get a coffee!

M2: I’ll make sure I get his attention! One last thing. I heard about one person called Brian White. Do you know anything about him?

M1: I can’t tell you anything about him.

M2: Why’s that?

M1: Because that’s me!
